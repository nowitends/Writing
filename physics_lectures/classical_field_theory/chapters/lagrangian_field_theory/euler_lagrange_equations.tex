\section{Euler--Lagrange Equations for a Scalar Field}

Suppose the Lagrangian density depends on a scalar field and its first
derivatives:
\[
    \mathcal{L}=\mathcal{L}(\phi,\partial_\mu\phi,x).
\]
The stationary action condition gives the field equation
\[
    \boxed{
    \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi}
    -
    \partial_\mu
    \left(
        \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}
        {\partial(\partial_\mu\phi)}
    \right)
    =0.
    }
\]
This is the field-theoretic Euler--Lagrange equation.

For several fields \(\phi^a\), there is one equation for each field component:
\[
    \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi^a}
    -
    \partial_\mu
    \left(
        \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}
        {\partial(\partial_\mu\phi^a)}
    \right)
    =0.
\]

The result is usually a partial differential equation rather than an ordinary
differential equation.
