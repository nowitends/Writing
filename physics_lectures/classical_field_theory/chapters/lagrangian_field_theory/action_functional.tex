\section{The Action Functional}

For one real scalar field in flat spacetime, a common form of the action is
\[
    S[\phi]
    =
    \int \mathcal{L}\bigl(\phi,\partial_\mu\phi,x\bigr)\,\dd^4x.
\]
Here \(\partial_\mu\phi\) denotes the first partial derivatives of the field,
and \(\dd^4x\) denotes the spacetime volume element in the chosen coordinates.

The action is a functional: it assigns a number to an entire field
configuration. The variational principle states that the physical field
configuration makes the action stationary under small variations that vanish on
the boundary.

\begin{definitionbox}
An \emph{action functional} in classical field theory is a map
\[
    \phi \mapsto S[\phi]
\]
from field configurations to real numbers, usually written as an integral of a
local Lagrangian density.
\end{definitionbox}
