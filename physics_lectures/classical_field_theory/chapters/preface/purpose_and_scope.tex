\section*{Purpose and Scope}

These notes are a working book on classical field theory. The central idea is
to treat fields as physical degrees of freedom distributed over space and time,
and to derive their equations from variational principles.

The intended path is gradual. We begin with the meaning of a field, then move to
Lagrangian field theory, symmetries, conservation laws, and standard examples
such as scalar fields, electromagnetism, and continuum models. Later chapters
can connect the same language to relativistic notation and gauge theory.

\begin{remarkbox}
This book should be developed chapter by chapter. Each new topic should be
added as a small source file, compiled, reviewed in the PDF, and then improved
in the next step.
\end{remarkbox}
