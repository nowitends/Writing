\section{Why Symmetries Matter}

A symmetry is a transformation that changes the description of a system without
changing the physical content of the action. In field theory, symmetries are not
only aesthetic. They determine conserved quantities and strongly restrict the
form of possible field equations.

Spatial translation symmetry is related to momentum conservation. Time
translation symmetry is related to energy conservation. Rotational symmetry is
related to angular momentum conservation. Internal symmetries lead to conserved
charges.

The power of the Lagrangian formulation is that these connections can be stated
systematically.
