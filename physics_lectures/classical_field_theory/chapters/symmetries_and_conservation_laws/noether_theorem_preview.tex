\section{Preview of Noether's Theorem}

Noether's theorem says that continuous symmetries of the action produce
conserved currents. In field theory this means that a symmetry leads to a
current \(j^\mu\) satisfying a local conservation law
\[
    \partial_\mu j^\mu = 0.
\]
This equation expresses conservation locally. For example, it can imply that a
total charge
\[
    Q(t)=\int_{\mathbb{R}^3} j^0(t,\vect{x})\,\dd^3x
\]
is independent of time, provided suitable boundary conditions hold.

\begin{summarybox}
Classical field theory is built from fields, actions, variational principles,
and symmetries. Noether's theorem is the bridge between symmetry and
conservation.
\end{summarybox}
