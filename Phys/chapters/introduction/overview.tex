These notes present selected foundations of physics with an emphasis on mathematical structure, conceptual clarity, and model-based reasoning. The course starts from mathematical tools and then develops the language of motion, forces, oscillations, waves, electromagnetic phenomena, measurements, cosmology, relativity, and modern physics.

Physics uses mathematics to describe patterns in nature, but the meaning of a formula depends on the assumptions behind the model. For this reason, the notes aim to combine equations with interpretation: what quantities represent, how they are measured, when a model applies, and how different parts of the theory fit together.
